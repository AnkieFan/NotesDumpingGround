## Code of Professional Practice for Drug Analysts
+ **Professional Conduct**: Analysts must work with integrity, impartiality, and scientific rigor. Safeguards against bias, conflict of interest, and malpractice are emphasized.
+ **Casework**: Requires proper handling, documentation, and retention of drug evidence; analysts must maintain contemporaneous notes enabling peer review.
+ **Reporting**: Reports and testimony must be objective, accurate, and transparent. Analysts must be willing to revise opinions in light of new evidence, and act if a miscarriage of justice is suspected .

## Education and Training
+ **Minimum Education**: Analysts should hold at least a bachelor’s degree in natural/physical sciences, including coursework in general, organic, and analytical chemistry .

### Training Requirements:
+ Initial training: covers drug chemistry, instrumentation, quality assurance, ethics, courtroom testimony, and safety .
+ Continuing professional development: minimum 20 hours annually, documented and relevant to the laboratory’s mission
+ Reference Materials: Analysts must have access to essential reference texts, spectral libraries, peer-reviewed journals, and legislation

### Methods of Analysis
1. Sampling Seized Drugs
   1. Sampling is designed to represent populations (e.g., net weight, prevalence of controlled substance in exhibits).
   2. Both statistical (probability-based) and non-statistical schemes are recognized; choice depends on jurisdiction and case requirements .
   3. Documentation of strategy, plan, and procedure is mandatory.
2. Analytical Scheme for Drug Identification
   1. **Core Principle**: Drug identification requires at least** two techniques from different categories **(A, B, C), one being **a Category A technique** (e.g., mass spectrometry, IR spectroscopy) .
   2. **Botanicals**: Cannabis and plant-based drugs may be identified morphologically by trained botanists under certain conditions .
   3. **Quality Practices**: Data must be reviewable and traceable (spectra, chromatograms, photographs).
3. Clandestine Laboratory Evidence
   1. Provides protocols for analyzing chemicals, intermediates, and equipment seized from illicit labs.
   2. Stresses safety, yield/capacity calculations, and specialized training in clandestine synthesis routes .
4. Analogues & Structural Class Determination
   1. Laboratories must have written protocols for identifying analogues and legally defined structural classes.
   2. Reports must clearly state limitations and assumptions; conclusions must remain within the analyst’s competence .

## Quality Assurance
1. General Practices
   + Establish a documented quality management system .
   + Evidence Control: Chain of custody, packaging, integrity checks, storage, and documentation retention are mandatory .
   + Analytical Procedures: Validation of methods, monitoring of reference materials, and case documentation (with spectra/graphs) are required
   + Proficiency Testing: Regular proficiency and competency testing for staff
2. Validation of Analytical Methods
   + Validation confirms a method is fit for purpose in real casework conditions .
   + **Parameters**: accuracy, precision, limit of detection, limit of quantitation, linearity, robustness, ruggedness, and uncertainty
   + Each laboratory must verify published or transferred methods internally.
3. Measurement Uncertainty
   + Uncertainty must be considered in both qualitative and quantitative work
   + Sources: weighing, purity determination, instrument performance
   + Reporting uncertainty is critical to demonstrate reliability and fitness for purpose

## Forensic Significance
+ Scientific Integrity: Recommendations provide a defensible basis for expert testimony, reducing the risk of wrongful convictions or acquittals.
+ Global Consistency: Adoption across jurisdictions ensures harmonization of drug analysis practices.
+ Adaptability: Recognizes technological advances and evolving drug landscapes (e.g., NPS, analogues).
+ Accreditation Alignment: Designed to integrate with ISO/IEC 17025 and ASTM standards .
