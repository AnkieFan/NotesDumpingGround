## Individualization -Illicit Drugs
### Case Circumstances:
Person H accuses his friend W of deliberately spiking his cigarette with cocaine in order to create dependency and later sell him more drugs. During the investigation, white powder is recovered from H’s cigarette box. A search of W’s home reveals multiple bags of cocaine, but W claims they are for his personal use and that the powder in H’s cigarette box originated from a party.

### Inference Type:
Individualization — to determine whether the cocaine found in H’s cigarette box can be linked specifically to the cocaine seized from W’s residence.

### Chemical Trace Evidence & Reference Material:
+ Trace: White powder from H’s cigarette box.
+ Reference: Cocaine samples seized at W’s house.
+ splitless GC + full-scan MS + SIM to confirmation/quant
+ Comparison: 
  + Impurity: ecgonine methyl ester, tropacocaine, cinnamoylcocaine, benzoic acid;
  + Cutting agents (levamisole, lidocaine, phenacetin, caffeine, procaine) and their peak-area ratios to cocaine
  + Semi-quant concentration via 6-point calibration (e.g., 0.01–10 µg mL⁻¹)
  + he resulting profile is compared to W’s seized bags with cosine/Pearson similarity and likelihood ratios.
+ Limitation: Interpretation needs reference data/QC and expert evaluation; analysis time and cost are non-trivial.

# Association (MDMA / Ecstasy)
## Case circumstances:
At a nightclub, police find multiple MDMA tablets on the floor. Later, they seize pills from a suspect’s pocket that look visually similar. The suspect claims his tablets came from a legal supplement and denies ownership of the seized ecstasy.

## Inference type:
Association – Are the MDMA tablets from the suspect chemically consistent with those recovered at the crime scene?

## Chemical trace evidence & reference:
+ Trace: Pills collected from nightclub floor.
+ Reference: Pills seized from suspect.

### Explanation of investigation:
By comparing chemical composition (MDMA alkaloid, excipients, synthesis impurities), it can be determined if both sets of pills are chemically consistent with each other. Shared impurity profiles would support an association (same batch/source).

### Analytical techniques:
+ GC–MS / LC–MS → identification of MDMA, related analogues (MDA, MDEA).
+ IRMS → isotope ratios for source profiling.

# Reconstruction (Heroin)
## Case circumstances:
During a raid in a warehouse, investigators find: (1) a container of brown heroin base, (2) a cutting agent (caffeine + paracetamol), and (3) packaged heroin powder prepared for street sale. Two suspects are present; both deny preparing drugs, claiming they only stored them.

## Inference type:
Reconstruction – What was the sequence of preparation?

## Chemical trace evidence & reference:
+ Raw heroin base (container).
+ Cutting agents in separate bags.
+ Prepared heroin/cutting agent mixtures (street-level packets).

### Explanation of investigation:
Analysis shows raw heroin in the container chemically matches the active ingredient in street packets. Caffeine/paracetamol levels are consistent with mixing ratios. The progression from pure base → mixing → packaging indicates active preparation for distribution, reconstructing the handling sequence.


## Analytical techniques:
+ GC–MS / LC–MS for alkaloids (heroin, morphine, codeine).
+ HPLC for paracetamol/caffeine adulterants.
+ Chemometric profiling to reconstruct mixing sequence.
