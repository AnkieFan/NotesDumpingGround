# Graph
 
- vertices V=
- edges E
- graph G

e.g.:
V = {x, y, z}
Edges:{x, y}, {x, z}, {y, z}
E = {{x, y}, {x, z}, {y, z}}

## Drawing a graph
G = <V, E>
V = {a, b, c, d}
E = {{a,c}, {c,d}}}

## vocabulary
+ neighbor
+ adjacent (vertices)
+ edge $e \in E$ is **incident** to a vertex $v \in V$ iff $v \in e
+ degree
+ $\delta(G) := min \{d(v)|v \in V\}$ minimum degree of G
+ $\Delta(G) := max \{d(v)|v \in V\}$ maximum degree of G
+ $d(G) = \frac{\sum_{v \in V}d(v)}{|v|}$
+ weights/labels: distance, time...
